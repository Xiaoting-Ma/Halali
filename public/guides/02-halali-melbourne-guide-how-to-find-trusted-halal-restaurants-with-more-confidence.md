## FAQ

### 1. Why is a Melbourne guide useful if availability language is Sydney-first?

Because Melbourne users still need a decision framework today. The guide is designed to reduce uncertainty by showing what strong halal trust signals look like, even when public live-city messaging remains Sydney-first. It is about better evaluation, not overclaiming product coverage.

### 2. Should I start with restaurant reviews?

Reviews can help, but they are not enough on their own. A review usually tells you whether food tasted good or delivery was fast. It does not reliably explain halal standard, mixed-kitchen policy, or whether the information behind the listing was reviewed and updated.

### 3. Why should I care whether the platform explains "full-halal"?

Because vague wording creates false confidence. If "full-halal" is never defined, users are forced to guess what the platform means. A platform that explains the term clearly gives you a much better foundation for comparing options responsibly.

### 4. What is the risk of using only halal tags?

The main risk is treating a discovery shortcut as proof. Tags may help surface choices, but they rarely explain kitchen setup, certificate currency, or the platform's own review standard. That means the user still carries most of the trust burden.

### 5. Why does city scope need to be stated clearly?

Clear scope is part of trust. If a platform is live in one city, it should say so directly instead of implying broader coverage. Transparent scope helps users understand what is operational today and what content is educational or future-facing.

### 6. How does mixed-kitchen information affect the decision?

It matters because many Muslim users are evaluating the restaurant environment, not only the item description. If the platform never explains mixed-kitchen policy, then an important trust question stays unresolved. That can make even a convenient listing feel uncertain.

### 7. Is this guide mainly for families?

No. It helps families, students, professionals, and new residents alike. The shared need is a lower-risk decision process. Different users may weigh the checklist differently, but they all benefit from clearer standards and fewer hidden assumptions.

### 8. What should I do after reading this guide?

Turn the checklist into your own filter. Read the Core Trust Page, compare platforms against the same trust questions, and shortlist only the options that explain standards, verification, and kitchen practice clearly. Confidence improves when the method is consistent.
