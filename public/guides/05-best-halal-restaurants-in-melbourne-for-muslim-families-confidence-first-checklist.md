## FAQ

### 1. Why should families in Melbourne focus on standards before reviews?

Because standards answer the trust question more directly than reviews do. Reviews can describe taste and service, but they rarely explain full-halal status, kitchen practice, or platform verification. Families benefit more from clear standards than from generic popularity signals.

### 2. What is the biggest mistake families make when choosing?

The most common mistake is treating a restaurant list as a trust list. A list may be helpful for discovery, but discovery and confidence are not the same thing. Families should narrow options by trust criteria before they compare menu or price.

### 3. Why does mixed-kitchen policy matter for group meals?

Because group organizers usually want fewer unresolved questions, not more. If mixed-kitchen practice is unclear, uncertainty remains at the exact point where families most want reassurance. A clear policy helps the decision feel more stable.

Not automatically. Broad wording is a reason to slow down and ask better questions, not a reason to jump to conclusions. The point is to match confidence to evidence, then choose the option with the clearest answers.

### 6. Why does "current information" matter so much?
